Best Overall 3-Season Tent: REI Co-op Half Dome 2 Plus

The REI Co-op Half Dome 2 Plus REI Co-op Half Dome 2 Plus is the tent most campers should buy first. It's durable, reasonably light at around 5 lbs 6 oz, and offers enough interior space that two people aren't elbowing each other all night. The nearly vertical walls create genuine livable volume, and the dual vestibules give each person their own gear storage area.

Setup is intuitive even in low light, thanks to color-coded clips and a freestanding design. The 40D rainfly performs well in moderate rain and wind, though you'll want to seam-seal it out of the box for best waterproofing. At this price, it's hard to argue with the value — REI's warranty and return policy also make it a low-risk purchase for first-time tent buyers.

Best for: Weekend backpackers, summer through early fall, couples or solo campers who want extra room.

Buying Advice: What Actually Matters

Before defaulting to the most expensive option, get honest about how you'll actually use the tent.

  • Season rating: Three-season tents cover most people's needs. Only buy four-season if you're camping in genuine winter conditions — they're heavier, more expensive, and unnecessarily hot in summer.

  • Capacity: Size down one person from the listed capacity if you want comfortable space. A "2-person" tent is snug for two; buy a 3-person if you want to move around.
  • Weight vs. durability: Ultralight tents demand more careful handling and setup. If you're new to backcountry camping, a mid-weight option like the Hubba Hubba 2 or Half Dome 2 Plus is more forgiving.
  • Floor dimensions vs. peak height: Check both. Some tents have generous floor space but low ceilings that feel claustrophobic sitting up.
  • Vestibule space: Especially important in rain — you need somewhere to stash muddy boots and wet gear that isn't inside the sleeping area.

Match the tent to the actual conditions you'll face, not the aspirational trip you imagine taking. A well-chosen mid-range tent used consistently beats an expensive tent that stays in the garage.
